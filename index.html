ASSISTIVE TECHNOLOGY HACKATHON

INFO

Welcome to DUNeS' Assistive Technology Hackathon!

Over the course of a weekend, developers, engineers, and people who have no idea what they're doing (like me) meet up and bring their ideas to life! That could be a prototype, a physical object, a computer program, or even just a presentation on a possible product! It doesn't matter whether you're here to build or code something interesting, get people thinking about technology's role in an equitable world for disabled people, or just have fun, you should sign up!

What is assistive technology?

Assistive tech is all about making the world a more inclusive and accessible place to those with disabilities, utilising technology to break down those barriers and lift up everyone in the process. This can be something as simple as a screen-reader for the visually impaired, or as advanced as a robotic arm for those with limited mobility. If it's technology to aid disabled people in their day-to-day life, its assistive tech!

Can you compete in teams?

Yes, you can! Sign-ups are encouraged to compete in teams or solo, depending on their own preference!

Where will this be held?

This event will be held in An Mheitheal, in Printing House Square. Check out our Instagram page for a walkthrough video on how to get to An Mheitheal/Printing House Square or email us for a written description/assistive guidance

ACCESSIBILITY INFO

PHYSICAL ACCESSIBILITY

This event is fully wheelchair accessible, and attendees are more than welcome to work on their projects from home. There will be an accessible, gender-neutral toilet available.

There will be a number of different types of seating available: hard chairs, softer cushioned chairs, and bean bags.

We are currently in discussion with the DS to obtain a HEPA filter for this event but this cannot be confirmed yet. We do encourage masking and antigen testing beforehand where possible. We ask that you do not attend in person if you are newly unwell and instead you are welcome to join the hackathon from home.

SENSORY INFO

There will be slightly dimmed lights, some loud noise, and the room may be crowded and have lots of chatter. There will be stim toys and a quiet room available, however the quiet room is not physically accessible due to 3 steps leading to the room.

The temperature of the room is adjustable so please approach a committee member if the room is getting stuffy or too cold.

There may be stronger smells when the food is served at lunchtime.

You are welcome to step out at any point and are not obligated to be there for the full time of the event.

We will have a very limited number of ear defenders on hand.

GENERAL ACCESSIBILITY AND ACCOMODATIONS

Committee members will have name tags, please approach a committee member if there are any issues, concerns, or questions that you have during the event.

If you have any further accessibility requests, including ISL interpretation, walkthrough videos, or further explanation, please contact us using one of the methods below, and we will try our best to accomodate you!
